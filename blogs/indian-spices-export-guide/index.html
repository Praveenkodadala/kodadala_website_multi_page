India's Spice Export Market

India is the world's largest producer, consumer, and exporter of spices — accounting for nearly 25% of global spice trade. The country exports over 1 million metric tonnes annually, generating more than USD 4 billion in revenue.

Regulations and Compliance

To export spices from India you need several registrations. The most important are IEC (Import Export Code) from DGFT, e-RCMC from Spices Board of India, FSSAI central licence, and GST registration. Exporting without IEC is illegal.

Quality Standards

International buyers primarily require compliance with FSSAI standards (India), EU Maximum Residue Limits (MRLs), US FDA import alerts, and Gulf Standard Organisation (GSO) requirements. Lab testing for pesticide residues, heavy metals, aflatoxins, and microbial contamination is standard.

Logistics and Shipping

Most Indian spices are shipped via sea freight (FCL or LCL) from major ports: Chennai, Mumbai (JNPT), Mundra, Vizag, and Kolkata. Air freight is used for high-value or time-sensitive orders. Lead times typically range from 3-5 weeks for sea freight depending on destination.

Finding Buyers

The most effective channels for finding international buyers are Spices Board buyer-seller meets, participation in trade fairs (Anuga, Gulfood, SIAL), B2B platforms (Alibaba, IndiaMART, Trade India), and direct outreach to import brokers in target markets.
